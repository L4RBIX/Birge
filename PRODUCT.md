# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Birge serves Kazakhstan shoppers who want lower prices on global marketplace goods by joining city-based buying groups. The primary demo audience is hackathon judges evaluating commerce execution, telecom trust, and anti-fraud mechanics.

## Product Purpose
Birge is a Kazakhstan group-buying marketplace: "Собери группу — получи опт." People join live deals, unlock wholesale price tiers together, pay in KZT, and share QR/join links to fill the group before the countdown ends.

Core mechanics:
- Live group deals with city pools for Алматы and Астана.
- Wholesale price tiers, countdowns, progress targets, cargo costs, and savings shown before joining.
- Escrow mock: money is held while the group forms, auto-refunded if the group does not close, then sent to cargo consolidation after closing.
- QR live demo and sharing flow for WhatsApp, Telegram, and copied links.
- SIM trust layer: "1 SIM = 1 место", SIM verification, duplicate-join blocking, operator trust, and anti-bot proof.

Birge must not be positioned as AI demand aggregation, AI link parsing, or demand pooling. AI is not the core product pitch.

## Brand Personality
Premium, practical, trusted. Birge should feel like a serious luxury-commerce surface adapted for Kazakhstan group buying, not a playful hackathon MVP.

## Anti-references
Do not make Birge look like a generic SaaS landing page, dark dashboard, neon startup template, AI product page, abstract illustration site, or card-heavy pitch deck.

## Design Principles
- Preserve the premium commerce foundation before adding new mechanics.
- Show group-buy economics directly: retail, wholesale, cargo, final price, and savings.
- Make SIM trust visible at the exact moment it affects the buyer.
- Keep hackathon demo flows stable, local-state driven, and screenshot-ready.
- Localize for Kazakhstan with KZT pricing, city context, RU/KZ language affordance, and telecom trust.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-reachable controls, visible focus states, 44px minimum touch targets, reduced-motion fallbacks, and readable layouts at 390px mobile and 1440px desktop.
